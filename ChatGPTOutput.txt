Infrastructure: Requires Google and Amazon to build local cloud data centers within Israel, ensuring that data is stored and processed domestically.

Security & sovereignty: Designed to give the government control over data residency and prevent foreign access or shutdowns.

How was Google founded?

Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were PhD students at Stanford University. Here’s the short, accurate story:

1. It began as a research project (1995–1996)

Larry Page came to Stanford for grad school; Sergey Brin was assigned to show him around.

They began collaborating on a research idea: using links between web pages as a way to measure a page’s importance.

The project was initially called “Backrub” because it analyzed backlinks on the internet.

2. They built the PageRank algorithm

Their key innovation was PageRank, an algorithm that ranked websites based on how many other important sites linked to them.

This produced far more relevant search results than existing search engines of the time.

3. They needed more computing power

They assembled a makeshift data center in their dorm with cheap hardware (including LEGO-encased servers).

Backrub became popular within Stanford, consuming so much bandwidth that administrators took notice.

4. “Google” is named after a math term

They renamed the project Google, a play on the word “googol” (10¹⁰⁰), reflecting their goal of organizing vast amounts of information.

5. Google becomes a company (1998)

On September 4, 1998, they incorporated Google, Inc.

They operated out of a garage in Menlo Park, California—rented from Susan Wojcicki (later YouTube’s CEO).

Early investors included Andy Bechtolsheim (co-founder of Sun Microsystems), who famously wrote a $100,000 check to “Google Inc.” before the company even formally existed.

6. Early success

With clean design, fast results, and superior relevancy, Google quickly outperformed other search engines like AltaVista, Yahoo Search, and Lycos.

By the early 2000s, it became the dominant search engine worldwide.
